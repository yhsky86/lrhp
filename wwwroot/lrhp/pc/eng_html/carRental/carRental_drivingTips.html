Driving Tips in Korea

Driving Tips in Jeju

Introducing LOTTE rent-a-car LOTTE rent-a-car runs the largest car rental business in Korea with over 220 branches and 210,000 cars. Over 30 years
of experience allow us to provide innovative and excellent service to our customers.

Car rental Precautions

  • Rental Requirements

    In order to rent a car, customer must provide passport, International Driving Permit (IDP) issued by countries under
    the Geneva and Vienna Conventions or Korean Driver’s license and credit card.

    • Grade B: Allowed to drive up to nine-seater vehicle
    • Grade D: Allowed to drive up to twelve-seater vehicle

    EU license holders must have IDP to drive in Korea.

  • Insurance

    The following is our insurance coverage:

    • Third Party Liability: Unlimited
    • Property damage: 20 million KRW
    • Personal Injuries: 15 million KRW

  • CDW (Collision Damage Waiver)

    CDW is an insurance program that protects you from full liability for damages to the car in the case of accident by paying certain amount
    or received full protection depending on the type of CDW program registered.

    3 types of CDW program include:

    • 50,000 KRW deductible
    • 300,000 KRW deductible
    • Unlimited (Full-coverage)

  • Compensation fee for Inoperable Vehicle

    In the case of accident, customer shall bear 50% of the standard rental fare corresponding to the period
    required for the vehicle to be repaired.

  • Refueling Policy

    The amount of fuel contained when pick-up will be recorded to calculate with the fuel left when return to determine whether customer
    will be refunded or charged for additional fee for refueling. Refueling cost will be based on the fuel chart placed on the front desk at the
    rental location.

  • Extension of Contract

    If you need to extend the rental period, please contact us prior to your return date. Otherwise, you may not be able
    to partially or entirely benefit from the insurance and other coverage.

  • Additional Driver

    There is no extra charge for additional driver. However, the additional driver must inform and sign the rental
    contract to be included in the insurance coverage.

Tips for Driving in Jeju

  • Speed Camera

    Please be aware of speed cameras while driving. Car GPS system will let you know when there is an upcoming speed camera. Make sure to not exceed the recommended speed.

  • Left Turn Signal

    Drivers must wait for the left turn signal to turn green before making a turn. In many cases, there will only be a left turn sign. This sign means you may turn left when the light turned green but you must yield to cars coming from the opposite side.

  • Roundabouts

    You must yield to the cars already inside the circle before entering. Some roundabouts have more than 4 roads connected; therefore, be aware to your right for which road you need to enter.

  • Driving in Fog

    With the humid and warm environment of Jeju Island, roads at night could be covered with fog. Please be careful and utilize the car lights when driving in a foggy weather.

  • School Zone

    If you see the "School Zone" sign, be aware that there is a school located nearby. Please drive slowly under 30 km/h and pay attention to students crossing the street.

  • Parking

    Street parking is usually not allowed unless parking spot is marked only by white line. If there are numbers beside the parking spot, this indicates that the parking spot is only for local residents.

  • Fine

    Fine for the violation of traffic law will be charged through LOTTE rental.
    LOTTE rental will charge additional fee to customers for any fines.
